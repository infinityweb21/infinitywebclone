Toronto

The Crown Jewel of Canada

The most popular city in the country, located on the shores of Lake Ontario, is filled with culture and you're greeted with a skyline that speaks volumes and crowded neighborhoods filled with life. The beauty lies in its diversity with over 140 languages spoken here making it one of the most multicultural cities in the world where you can have dim sum in Chinatown for lunch, sip espresso in a cozy Italian café by afternoon and enjoy spicy Caribbean flavors for dinner. Enjoy a show in the Entertainment District, browse indie galleries in Queen West or see the Royal Ontario Museum.

The city offers peaceful parks, lakeside trails and quick getaways to the Toronto Islands. Even when the cold sets in the city transforms into a winter wonderland with ice rinks, festive markets and a unique underground walkway system known as the path that is perfect for exploring the downtown core. Whether you’re looking for an adventure or simply in need of a fresh perspective, Toronto welcomes you with open arms. This city is always the right choice no matter the season or the reason.

Distillery Historic District

It is a pedestrian-only area filled with cobblestone streets, art galleries, boutique shops and trendy restaurants. It's the perfect place to enjoy some culture and history with a relaxing afternoon walk. This area also hosts seasonal events making it unique throughout the year.

Toronto Islands

A short ferry ride from downtown offers an escape from the busy city life where you can enjoy scenic views of the skyline, relax on the beaches, rent bikes or have a picnic in one of the many green spaces. It’s an ideal spot for outdoor lovers looking for a mini getaway within the city.

St. Lawrence Market

A crowded marketplace where you can discover local flavors, artisan products and a piece of the city’s culinary history. It’s a place with vendors selling everything from fresh produce to gourmet items and don’t forget to try the iconic peameal bacon sandwich.

Casa Loma

This is the city’s majestic castle located on a hill, overlooking the city and was built in the early 20th century. This Gothic Revival mansion is full of grand halls, secret passages and offers a unique experience whether you’re into history or architecture.

High Park

The largest public park of the city and a green haven for nature lovers featuring walking trails, gardens, a zoo and even a pond. It’s especially popular for its cherry blossom trees in the spring. The location is a perfect urban escape whether you're looking to relax or have a family picnic.

Hockey Hall of Fame

A must-visit destination for sports lovers, especially for fans of Canada's beloved national game located in a beautiful historic building downtown offering interactive exhibits, memorabilia and even a chance to see the Stanley Cup up close. It's a must-visit for anyone interested in hockey and Canadian culture.
