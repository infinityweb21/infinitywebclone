Flight-Saving Hacks You Need

  • 1. Book in advance: Reserving tickets 3–4 weeks ahead often results in lower prices.
  • 2. Fly budget airlines: Low-cost carriers can cut your travel expenses significantly.
  • 3. Redeem rewards: Use points from credit cards or airline programs to save on fares.
  • 4. Talk to an agent: Travel agents may offer special discounts and lower booking fees.
  • 5. Stay flexible: Changing your dates or destination could unlock better flight deals.
  • 6. Join loyalty programs: These are free and let you earn rewards toward future flights.
  • 7. Set fare alerts: Sign up for notifications to track discounts and price changes fast.
  • 8. Try different currencies: Booking in foreign currency can sometimes offer lower prices.
  • 9. Combine carriers: One-way tickets with multiple airlines may be cheaper than round trips.

Your Guide to Low-Fare Days

Though it depends on your route, midweek flights, especially between Monday and Thursday, are typically cheaper. Fridays and weekends often cost more because of increased demand. Steering clear of holiday periods and peak travel seasons can also help you snag better deals.
